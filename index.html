Thank you for visiting my website. Here, I post my thoughts on data science and HCI; summaries of papers I've read and talks I've watched; and reflections on other things that I read.

[Paper] Data and Power: Archival Appraisal Theory as a Framework for Data Preservation

2020-11-13 1080 words papers, reading club, cscw2020, Sarita Y Schoenebeck, Paul Conway

Another [[CSCW]] paper for this week’s reading group; this one on digital archives. What does data preservation mean in a digital world? How do adults see their legacy? This paper draws on archival theory to study these questions.

[Paper] 'At the End of the Day Facebook Does What It Wants': How Users Experience Contesting Algorithmic Content Moderation

2020-11-08 1096 words papers, cscw2020, Kristen Vaccaro, Christian Sandvig, Karrie G Karahalios

When algorithms make decisions, humans should be able to appeal them. Right? This CSCW paper studies users’ experience with contesting algorithmic content moderation decisions.

[Paper] The Hardware Lottery

2020-11-02 515 words papers, reading club, Sara Hooker

The “hardware lottery” is when a research idea succeeds because it is suitable with available hardware. What does this mean for the future of research?

My review of Calling Bullshit

2020-10-31 776 words books,

“The world is awash with bullshit,” Carl Bergstrom and Jevin West write. What is it? How do we recognize it? How do we call it out when we see it? Calling Bullshit: The Art of Skepticism in a Data-Driven World, informs us on these questions.

[Paper] Hidden Figures: Roles and Pathways of Successful OSS Contributors

2020-10-24 885 words papers, cscw2020, Bianca Trinkenreich, Mariam Guizani, Igor Wiese, Anita Sarma, Igor Steinmacher

This CSCW paper studies open source software contributors—all of them, not just maintainers and developers. While open source has often been looked at as a purely technical community, this paper draws attention to the other forms of work that contributors do.

[Paper] Mitigating Exploitation: Indie Game Developers' Reconfigurations of Labor in Technology

2020-10-23 1361 words papers, cscw2020, Guo Freeman, Jeffrey Bardzell, Shaowen Bardzell, Nathan Mcneese

In a totally different direction from the last two CSCW papers, this work studies the indie game development industry. How does the indie community reconfigure labor in their own image? This paper explores these questions in the context of labor and technology.
