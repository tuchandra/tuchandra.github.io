Thank you for visiting my website. Here, I post my thoughts on data science and HCI; summaries of papers I've read and talks I've watched; and reflections on other things that I read.

[Paper] Bayesian Multilevel Estimation with Poststratification: State-Level Estimates from National Polls

2019-11-26 856 words papers, David K. Park, Andrew Gelman, Joseph Bafumi

This is a summary of the seminal paper by David K. Park, Andrew Gelman, and Joseph Bafumi. View the original on Gelman’s website.

[Paper] A Taxonomy of Ethical Tensions in Inferring Mental Health States from Social Media (part 2/2)

2019-11-25 768 words papers, Stevie Chancellor, Michael Birnbaum, Eric Caine, Vince Silenzio, Munmun De Choudhury

Part 2/2 of the FAT* 2019 paper by Dr. Chancellor et al., studying all the various ethical tensions with inferring mental health states from social media. This post focuses on the calls to action and my reflections. View part 1 of the summary here.

[Paper] A Taxonomy of Ethical Tensions in Inferring Mental Health States from Social Media (part 1/2)

2019-11-24 1515 words papers, Stevie Chancellor, Michael Birnbaum, Eric Caine, Vince Silenzio, Munmun De Choudhury

Research shows that it’s possible to infer individuals’ mental health status from social media. While this has great potential for good, it also has great potential for misuse. So much, in fact, that this FAT* 2019 paper creates a taxonomy of all the different ethical tensions with this! This is part 1 of 2.

On the digital ad industry

2019-11-21 1324 words general,

I recently read a fantastic article on The Correspondent about digital advertising, called The new dot com bubble is here: it’s called online advertising. This post summarizes my thoughts.

[Paper] A Bayesian Cognition Approach to Improve Data Visualization

2019-11-20 1319 words papers, Yea-Seul Kim, Logan A Walls, Peter Krafft, Jessica Hullman

Data visualization is common for communicating information, but how do people interpret visualizations? How do they reconicle information presented to them with their prior beliefs? The authors study a Bayesian cognition approach in this CHI 2019 paper.

[Paper] Model Cards for Model Reporting

2019-11-17 1169 words papers, Margaret Mitchell, Simone Wu, Andrew Zaldivar, Parker Barnes, Lucy Vasserman, Ben Hutchinson, Elena Spitzer, Inioluwa Deborah Raji, Timnit Gebru

What kinds of documentation should exist for machine learning models? This paper proposes one idea called “model cards” for reporting models’ performance, limitations, expected usage, and more, with a goal of minimizing their misuse.

Using papermill to wrap model runs

2019-11-15 720 words general,

I recently started using papermill from nteract to parameterize model runs. I’ve started to like it a lot, and this post will discuss my experiences with it and my current setup.

This one is a psychology paper by Cindy Xiong, Lisanne van Weelden, and Steven Franconeri. View it on the Northwestern Psychology department.
